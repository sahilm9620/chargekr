EV Charging Stations in India: The Complete Guide 2026

March 6, 2026 · 8 min read

The State of EV Charging Infrastructure in India

India's electric vehicle revolution is accelerating at an unprecedented pace. With over 39,000 public EV charging stations operational by the end of 2025, the country is rapidly building the infrastructure needed to support the growing fleet of electric vehicles on its roads. The Indian EV market, valued at over $54 billion in 2025, is projected to grow at a CAGR of 49% through 2030.

Government initiatives like the FAME II scheme (Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Electric Vehicles) and state-level EV policies have been instrumental in driving this growth. Major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Chennai, and Hyderabad now have extensive charging networks, with new stations being added daily.

Types of EV Charging Stations Available

Understanding the different types of EV chargers is crucial for every EV owner in India:

Level 1 AC Charging (Slow): Uses a standard 230V home outlet. Adds 3-8 km of range per hour. Best for overnight home charging with zero installation cost. Perfect for daily commuters who drive less than 50 km/day.
Level 2 AC Charging (Moderate): Requires a dedicated 240V circuit with a wall-mounted EVSE unit. Adds 16-50 km per hour. The most popular choice for home and workplace charging in India, costing ₹15,000-₹80,000 to install.
DC Fast Charging (Rapid): High-powered stations that deliver 50-350 kW of DC power directly to the battery. Can charge to 80% in 20-40 minutes. Found along highways and in commercial areas. Uses CCS Combo 2 or CHAdeMO connectors.

EV Range Anxiety in India: How to Overcome It in 2026

March 6, 2026 · 7 min read

What Is Range Anxiety and Why It's India's Biggest EV Barrier

Range anxiety the fear of your electric vehicle running out of battery before reaching a charging station is consistently cited as the #1 reason Indians hesitate to buy EVs. A 2025 survey found that 67% of potential EV buyers listed range anxiety as their primary concern, even though most daily commutes in India average only 25-35 km.

The irony? Modern EVs like the Tata Nexon EV Max (437 km range), MG ZS EV (461 km), and Hyundai Ioniq 5 (631 km) offer more than enough range for daily use. The real issue isn't battery range — it's charging accessibility and awareness.

7 Practical Tips to Beat Range Anxiety

1. Know Your Real Range: Your EV's rated range is tested under ideal conditions. In Indian traffic with AC running, expect 70-85% of the rated range. A 400 km rated EV gives you 280-340 km of real-world range still more than enough for most trips.
2. Charge at Home Overnight: Plug in every night, just like you charge your phone. You'll start each day with a full battery, covering 200+ km. Learn about home charger options →
3. Use Chargekr for Trip Planning: Our app shows all available chargers (public + peer-to-peer) along your route. Plan stops in advance and never worry about finding a charger. See how Chargekr helps →
4. Charge to 80%, Not 100%: Charging from 20-80% is the fastest (the last 20% is slow). DC fast chargers can do this in 20-40 minutes barely longer than a coffee break.
5. Drive Efficiently: Use regenerative braking, maintain steady speeds, and use eco mode. These simple habits can extend your range by 15-20%.
6. Join the P2P Charging Network: Chargekr's peer-to-peer network means there are chargers in residential neighborhoods, not just highways. Your neighbor might have a charger you can use. Explore P2P charging →
7. Check Real-Time Availability: Before heading to a charger, use the Chargekr app to check real-time availability and book a slot in advance. No more arriving at a busy station with no open charger.

India's Charging Network Is Growing Fast

The charging landscape is transforming rapidly. India went from 2,000 public charging stations in 2022 to over 39,000 by end of 2025 a 19x increase in just 3 years. Add in Chargekr's growing network of peer-to-peer chargers and the total number of available charging points is even higher.

Major highway corridors like Delhi-Jaipur, Mumbai-Pune, Bengaluru-Mysuru, and Chennai-Pondicherry now have fast chargers every 50-100 km. Urban areas have charging stations at malls, offices, and apartment complexes. The range anxiety of 2022 is becoming the range confidence of 2026.
