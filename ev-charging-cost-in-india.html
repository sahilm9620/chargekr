Cost Guide

EV Charging Cost in India

Learn how much it costs to charge an EV in India at home, on public chargers, and with fast charging.

Charging at home

Home charging is often the cheapest option. With a dedicated Level 2 charger, most EV owners in India pay around ₹6-10 per kWh. This makes overnight charging the best choice for daily commuters.

Public AC charging

Public AC chargers typically cost ₹12-18 per kWh in Indian cities. They are a good mid-range choice for local top-ups during the day when you cannot charge at home.

DC fast charging

DC fast chargers deliver energy faster but at a premium. Rates are usually ₹18-25 per kWh depending on the station and location. For highway travel and quick stops, this cost is often worth the time saved.

Peer-to-peer charging

Peer-to-peer charging on ChargeKr can be more affordable than commercial fast charging. Many hosts price shared chargers between ₹8-15 per kWh, making this a strong option for drivers who want convenience without high fast-charger rates.

How to save on charging

Charge at night: Overnight home charging is usually the most economical.
Compare prices: Use apps to compare public and peer-to-peer rates before you plug in.
Plan your route: Avoid expensive on-route chargers by booking in advance when possible.
